
METRO HEALTH CENTRE — ANNUAL PERFORMANCE REPORT 2023
Specialist Cardiac and Oncology Centre | ISO 9001:2015 Certified

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
Metro Health Centre is a 480-bed specialist centre with internationally recognised programmes in cardiology and oncology. In 2023, the hospital recorded 35,600 inpatient admissions, 142,000 outpatient visits, and 48,400 emergency department attendances. Total annual revenue was USD 380 million, with operating expenditure of USD 358 million, yielding a net operating surplus of USD 22 million.

PATIENT OUTCOMES AND QUALITY METRICS
The overall 30-day all-cause readmission rate at Metro Health Centre in 2023 was 10.8%, an improvement from 12.3% in 2022 and well below the national benchmark of 15.2%. Heart failure readmissions were 16.2%, representing best performance in the regional network. Cardiac surgery readmissions were 9.4% and oncology readmissions were 13.6%.
The in-hospital mortality rate was 1.8% of all admissions, the lowest among regional hospitals. Cardiac surgery mortality rate was 1.2% across 4,200 cardiac surgical procedures. ICU mortality rate was 12.4% across 2,600 ICU admissions.
Patient safety events: 2.6 falls per 1,000 patient days, 0.7 hospital-acquired infections per 1,000 patient days, and 0.3 medication errors per 100 admissions. All three metrics are among the top quartile regionally.
Patient satisfaction scores averaged 91.2 out of 100 on the HCAHPS survey, placing Metro Health Centre in the 92nd percentile nationally. Communication with doctors scored 94.6 and cleanliness of hospital scored 95.1.

STAFFING AND WORKFORCE
Metro Health Centre employed 3,100 FTE staff in 2023. Clinical staff included 480 physicians with a high concentration of cardiologists (82) and oncologists (64), 1,320 registered nurses, 240 allied health professionals, and 280 support staff. Administrative staff totalled 780 FTE.
The nurse-to-patient ratio in specialist wards was 1:4 during day shifts and 1:6 during night shifts. ICU and cardiac care unit nurse-to-patient ratio was 1:2. Physician-to-bed ratio was 1.00 physicians per bed, higher than regional average.
Staff turnover rate was 8.6% in 2023, the lowest in the regional health network. Nursing vacancy rate was 3.8%. Agency staff utilisation was 4.2% of total nursing hours, costing USD 5.8 million annually. Average length of employment was 9.2 years.
Staff training investment was USD 4.8 million representing 1,680 training hours per clinical FTE per year. Mandatory training compliance was 97.8%.

CLINICAL SERVICES AND CAPACITY
Metro Health Centre specialises in Cardiology, Cardiac Surgery, Interventional Cardiology, Oncology, Haematology, Radiation Oncology, and supporting departments including Anaesthesia, Pathology, Radiology, and Rehabilitation.
Bed occupancy rate was 88.4% in 2023. Average length of stay was 5.6 days, slightly higher than regional average reflecting the complexity of cardiac and oncology admissions. Surgical theatre utilisation was 86.2% across 8 specialist operating theatres.
Cardiac centre metrics: 5,800 cardiac catheterisation procedures, 4,200 open heart surgeries, 1,840 electrophysiology procedures, and 620 transcatheter aortic valve implantations (TAVI). Door-to-balloon time for STEMI averaged 52 minutes, among the best in the region.
Oncology centre treated 4,200 new cancer diagnoses in 2023. Cancer types: colorectal 18%, lung 15%, breast 14%, haematological 13%, prostate 9%, other 31%. Five-year cancer survival rates: breast 89.4%, colorectal 72.8%, lung 42.6%.

FINANCIAL PERFORMANCE
Total revenue: USD 380 million. Revenue breakdown: inpatient services 62%, outpatient services 22%, research 8%, emergency 6%, other 2%.
Operating expenditure: USD 358 million. Breakdown: staffing 58%, pharmaceuticals and high-cost drugs 22%, capital equipment 9%, facilities 6%, administrative 5%.
Cost per inpatient admission: USD 10,674, reflecting specialist complexity. Cost per cardiac procedure: USD 18,400. Cost per chemotherapy course: USD 24,600.
Capital investment: USD 42 million in 2023, including USD 22 million on new cardiac hybrid theatre, USD 12 million on proton therapy planning, and USD 8 million on digital pathology infrastructure.

INFECTION CONTROL AND PATIENT SAFETY
Hospital-acquired infection rate: 0.7 per 1,000 patient days, lowest in the region. MRSA bloodstream infection rate: 0.08 per 10,000 patient days. Clostridioides difficile rate: 0.24 per 10,000 patient days. CLABSI rate: 0.64 per 1,000 central line days.
Surgical site infection rate: 1.1% across clean surgical procedures. Hand hygiene compliance: 96.4%, highest in the regional network.
Antibiotic stewardship programme reduced broad-spectrum antibiotic use by 18% in 2023. Pharmacy review of high-risk medications completed within 24 hours for 99.4% of applicable cases.

RESEARCH AND EDUCATION
Metro Health Centre conducted 124 active clinical trials in 2023, including 18 Phase III trials and 42 Phase II trials, making it the most research-active hospital in the region. Research publications: 312 peer-reviewed papers. Research income: USD 30.4 million.
Affiliated with Metro Medical School, the centre trained 180 medical students, 240 cardiology and oncology specialty trainees, and 360 nursing students.
