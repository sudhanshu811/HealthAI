
CITY GENERAL HOSPITAL — ANNUAL PERFORMANCE REPORT 2023
Urban Tertiary Care Centre | Accredited: JCI Gold Standard

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
City General Hospital is a 650-bed urban tertiary care centre serving a population of 1.8 million across the metropolitan area. In 2023, the hospital recorded 48,200 inpatient admissions, 185,000 outpatient visits, and 72,000 emergency department attendances. Total annual revenue was USD 420 million, with operating expenditure of USD 395 million, yielding a net operating surplus of USD 25 million.

PATIENT OUTCOMES AND QUALITY METRICS
The overall 30-day all-cause readmission rate for City General Hospital in 2023 was 12.4%, a reduction from 14.1% in 2022 and significantly below the national benchmark of 15.2%. Heart failure readmissions were 18.6%, pneumonia readmissions were 11.2%, and COPD readmissions were 16.8%.
The in-hospital mortality rate was 2.1% of all admissions. Surgical mortality rate was 0.8% across 18,400 surgical procedures performed. The intensive care unit (ICU) mortality rate was 14.6% across 3,200 ICU admissions.
Patient safety events: 3.2 falls per 1,000 patient days, 0.9 hospital-acquired infections per 1,000 patient days, and 0.4 medication errors per 100 admissions. All three metrics showed improvement from 2022 baseline figures.
Patient satisfaction scores averaged 87.4 out of 100 on the standardised HCAHPS survey, placing City General Hospital in the 78th percentile nationally. Communication with nurses scored 91.2 and responsiveness of hospital staff scored 83.6.

STAFFING AND WORKFORCE
City General Hospital employed 4,200 full-time equivalent (FTE) staff in 2023. Clinical staff included 520 physicians across 38 specialties, 1,840 registered nurses, 280 allied health professionals, and 380 support staff. Administrative and operational staff totalled 1,180 FTE.
The nurse-to-patient ratio in general wards was 1:5 during day shifts and 1:7 during night shifts. ICU nurse-to-patient ratio was 1:2 at all times. Physician-to-bed ratio was 0.80 physicians per bed.
Staff turnover rate was 11.3% in 2023, down from 14.8% in 2022. Nursing vacancy rate was 6.2%. Agency staff utilisation was 8.4% of total nursing hours, costing USD 12.4 million annually. Average length of employment was 6.8 years.
Staff training investment was USD 3.2 million, representing 1,240 training hours per clinical FTE per year. Mandatory training compliance rate was 94.6%.

CLINICAL SERVICES AND CAPACITY
City General Hospital operates across 18 clinical departments including General Medicine, General Surgery, Cardiology, Oncology, Orthopaedics, Neurology, Obstetrics and Gynaecology, Paediatrics, Psychiatry, and Emergency Medicine.
Bed occupancy rate was 84.6% in 2023. Average length of stay (ALOS) was 4.8 days across all admissions, a reduction from 5.2 days in 2022. Surgical theatre utilisation was 78.3% across 12 operating theatres.
Emergency department (ED) metrics: average door-to-doctor time was 18 minutes, 4-hour wait compliance was 86.2%, and left without being seen rate was 2.1%. Total ED attendances were 72,000 with 24,800 resulting in hospital admission.
Cardiac catheterisation laboratory performed 3,200 procedures including 1,840 coronary angiograms, 980 percutaneous coronary interventions (PCI), and 380 electrophysiology studies. Door-to-balloon time for STEMI averaged 64 minutes, meeting international targets.

FINANCIAL PERFORMANCE
Total revenue: USD 420 million. Revenue breakdown: inpatient services 58%, outpatient services 24%, emergency services 11%, research grants 4%, other 3%.
Operating expenditure: USD 395 million. Expenditure breakdown: staffing 62%, pharmaceuticals and consumables 18%, capital equipment depreciation 8%, facilities and utilities 7%, administrative 5%.
Cost per inpatient admission: USD 8,706. Cost per emergency department visit: USD 642. Cost per outpatient visit: USD 544.
Capital investment in 2023: USD 28 million, including USD 14 million on digital health infrastructure, USD 8 million on surgical robotics, and USD 6 million on MRI and CT scanner upgrades.

INFECTION CONTROL AND PATIENT SAFETY
Hospital-acquired infection (HAI) rate: 0.9 per 1,000 patient days. MRSA bloodstream infection rate: 0.12 per 10,000 patient days. Clostridioides difficile infection rate: 0.38 per 10,000 patient days. Central line-associated bloodstream infection (CLABSI) rate: 0.82 per 1,000 central line days.
Surgical site infection rate: 1.4% across all clean surgical procedures. Hand hygiene compliance audited at 93.8%, up from 89.4% in 2022.
Medication reconciliation completion rate: 96.2% on admission, 91.8% on discharge. High-risk medication double-check compliance: 99.1%.

RESEARCH AND EDUCATION
City General Hospital conducted 84 active clinical trials in 2023, including 12 Phase III trials and 28 Phase II trials. Research publications: 186 peer-reviewed papers. Research income: USD 16.8 million from government grants and industry partnerships.
The hospital is affiliated with City Medical University and trained 240 medical students, 180 junior doctors, and 420 nursing students in 2023.
