
SUNRISE MEDICAL INSTITUTE — ANNUAL PERFORMANCE REPORT 2023
Centre of Excellence for Diabetes and Endocrinology | NABH Accredited

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
Sunrise Medical Institute is a 320-bed specialist institute recognised as a national centre of excellence for diabetes, endocrinology, and metabolic disorders. In 2023, the hospital recorded 22,400 inpatient admissions, 98,000 outpatient visits, and 28,600 emergency department attendances. Total annual revenue was USD 180 million, with operating expenditure of USD 168 million, yielding a net operating surplus of USD 12 million.

PATIENT OUTCOMES AND QUALITY METRICS
The overall 30-day all-cause readmission rate at Sunrise Medical Institute in 2023 was 9.6%, the lowest among all three hospitals and significantly below the national benchmark of 15.2%. Diabetes-related readmissions were 8.2%, heart failure readmissions were 14.8%, and endocrine disorder readmissions were 7.4%.
The in-hospital mortality rate was 1.4%, the lowest of the three hospitals. Surgical mortality rate was 0.5% across 7,200 procedures. ICU mortality rate was 10.8% across 1,400 ICU admissions.
Patient safety events: 2.1 falls per 1,000 patient days, 0.6 hospital-acquired infections per 1,000 patient days, and 0.2 medication errors per 100 admissions. These represent the best patient safety metrics across all three hospitals.
Patient satisfaction scores averaged 93.8 out of 100, placing Sunrise Medical Institute in the 97th percentile nationally. Overall experience scored 95.2 and care coordination scored 94.6.

STAFFING AND WORKFORCE
Sunrise Medical Institute employed 2,100 FTE staff in 2023. Clinical staff included 280 physicians with strong specialist representation in Endocrinology (48), Diabetology (36), Nephrology (24), and Ophthalmology (18). Nursing staff totalled 880 registered nurses, allied health 180, and support staff 200. Administrative staff totalled 560 FTE.
The nurse-to-patient ratio in general wards was 1:4 during day shifts and 1:6 during night shifts. ICU nurse-to-patient ratio was 1:2. Physician-to-bed ratio was 0.875, above regional average.
Staff turnover rate was 7.2% in 2023, the lowest across all three hospitals and one of the best in the national health system. Nursing vacancy rate was 2.4%. Agency staff utilisation was 2.8% of nursing hours, costing USD 2.2 million annually. Average length of employment was 11.4 years, indicating exceptional staff retention.
Staff training investment was USD 2.8 million representing 2,100 training hours per clinical FTE per year, the highest training intensity of all three hospitals. Mandatory training compliance was 99.2%.

CLINICAL SERVICES AND CAPACITY
Sunrise Medical Institute operates specialist services in Endocrinology and Diabetes, Metabolic Medicine, Nephrology, Ophthalmology (diabetic eye disease), Vascular Surgery (diabetic foot), Cardiology (diabetic heart disease), Renal Transplant, and Nutrition and Dietetics.
Bed occupancy rate was 81.2% in 2023. Average length of stay was 4.2 days, the shortest of the three hospitals. Surgical theatre utilisation was 74.6% across 6 operating theatres.
Diabetes centre metrics: 18,400 diabetes patients actively managed, 4,200 new diabetes diagnoses, 3,600 insulin pump initiations, 2,800 CGM (continuous glucose monitoring) set-ups, and 1,200 bariatric surgery procedures for diabetes remission. HbA1c below 7% achieved in 68.4% of diabetes patients.
Renal service: 420 renal transplants performed with 1-year graft survival rate of 96.8%. Dialysis centre managing 840 chronic dialysis patients.

FINANCIAL PERFORMANCE
Total revenue: USD 180 million. Revenue breakdown: inpatient services 52%, outpatient and clinics 32%, research 8%, emergency 5%, other 3%.
Operating expenditure: USD 168 million. Breakdown: staffing 55%, pharmaceuticals and devices 20%, capital equipment 10%, facilities 9%, administrative 6%.
Cost per inpatient admission: USD 8,036, lowest of the three hospitals. Cost per outpatient visit: USD 588. Cost per renal transplant: USD 28,400.
Capital investment: USD 18 million in 2023, including USD 8 million on artificial pancreas technology, USD 6 million on remote patient monitoring infrastructure, and USD 4 million on CGM integration systems.

INFECTION CONTROL AND PATIENT SAFETY
Hospital-acquired infection rate: 0.6 per 1,000 patient days, the lowest of all three hospitals. MRSA bloodstream infection rate: 0.06 per 10,000 patient days. Clostridioides difficile rate: 0.18 per 10,000 patient days. CLABSI rate: 0.52 per 1,000 central line days, best in region.
Surgical site infection rate: 0.9% across clean procedures. Hand hygiene compliance: 98.2%, highest of the three hospitals.
Diabetic foot ulcer prevention programme reduced lower limb amputations by 22% in 2023. Antibiotic stewardship reduced resistant organism isolation by 14%.

RESEARCH AND EDUCATION
Sunrise Medical Institute conducted 68 active clinical trials in 2023, including 8 Phase III and 22 Phase II trials, with a focus on novel diabetes therapies and renal protection. Research publications: 148 peer-reviewed papers. Research income: USD 14.4 million.
The institute runs a nationally recognised Fellowship in Diabetology and trained 120 medical students, 80 specialist endocrinology trainees, and 200 nursing students in 2023.
